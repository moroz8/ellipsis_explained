1. What is an Ellipse?

Imagine a circle that someone sat on. That's an ellipse! But there is a very special math rule it follows.

Instead of one center point (like a circle), an ellipse has two special spots called Foci (plural for Focus).

The String Trick: If you pin a string at both foci and trace around it with a pencil, you'll draw a perfect ellipse. Look closely—the total length of the string NEVER changes!

2. Gravity and Space Orbits

Johannes Kepler discovered a massive secret about our universe: Planets don't orbit in perfect circles. They orbit in ellipses!

And here's the best part: The Sun isn't in the center. The Sun sits exactly on one of the foci. The other focus is just empty space.

Circle-like (Earth) Stretched Orbit (Comet)

Kepler's Second Law: Watch how the planet suddenly speeds up when it gets close to the Sun, and slows down when it's far away. That's gravity pulling it in!
